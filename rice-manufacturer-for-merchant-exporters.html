Why merchant-exporter enquiries need a dedicated page

A merchant exporter is not searching like a retail customer. The requirement usually begins with a buyer, target country, port, quantity, product specification, packing format and commercial deadline. A generic contact page loses that context.

UrbanFresh provides a dedicated path for merchant exporters to start a mill-side conversation from Karnal. The goal is to identify commercial fit quickly and avoid repeated clarification on the basic fields.

UrbanFresh only confirms services, documentation support, confidentiality terms, trial feasibility, certifications, production capacity and timelines after reviewing the actual requirement. This page does not invent fixed commitments.

Information to collect from the overseas buyer

  • Destination country and, where known, destination port.
  • Rice variety, processing style and important quality parameters.
  • Approximate quantity, shipment timing and repeat potential.
  • Bulk or private-label pack size, material and artwork status.
  • Any destination-specific documentation or labelling requirement.

Products to discuss

Merchant-exporter enquiries can reference 1121, 1718, 1509 or Traditional Basmati. Current availability and processing options must be confirmed.

Buyer-brand packaging

For private-label orders, add the buyer’s brand position, target pack sizes, packaging material, design readiness, quantity per size and destination-market labelling needs. See the private-label enquiry guide.

Frequently asked

Merchant-exporter questions.

Will UrbanFresh quote without a destination country?

You can start the conversation, but a country and preferably a port make the quotation much more useful.

Can I request buyer-branded packaging?

Yes. Include the pack format, material, artwork status, quantity and destination. Feasibility is reviewed per requirement.

Can UrbanFresh guarantee a quotation time?

No universal response-time promise is published. A complete brief helps UrbanFresh evaluate and respond more efficiently.

Are confidentiality terms available?

State the confidentiality requirement at the beginning. Any agreement or customer-protection terms should be discussed and accepted explicitly before commercial information is shared.
