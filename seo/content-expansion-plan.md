# UrbanFresh search-intent expansion plan

The BI Agri Foods site demonstrates a broad search architecture: company pages, product families, individual processing variants, buyer-type pages, country pages and buyer-education articles. UrbanFresh should build the same kind of hierarchy only where it can publish distinct, accurate and commercially useful information.

## Level 1: commercial pillars — implemented

- Rice mill in Karnal — `index.html`
- Basmati rice manufacturer in India — `basmati-rice-manufacturer-india.html`
- Bulk basmati rice supplier — `products.html`
- Rice manufacturer for merchant exporters — `rice-manufacturer-for-merchant-exporters.html`
- Overseas bulk supply enquiries — `basmati-rice-exporter-india.html`
- Private-label basmati rice — `private-label.html`

## Level 2: product families — implemented

- 1121 Basmati
- 1718 Basmati
- 1509 Basmati
- Traditional Basmati

## Level 3: processing-variant pages — wait for product confirmation

Potential pages include 1121 Steam, 1121 Sella, 1121 Golden Sella and equivalent 1718/1509 pages. Create them only after the mill confirms that the variant is genuinely supplied and provides buyer-approved specifications. Each page must contain distinct product information, not a swapped keyword.

## Level 4: destination-market pages — wait for export evidence

Country pages can attract high-intent importers, but they need real destination-specific value: available documentation, residue or labelling considerations, port/logistics experience, packaging norms and a genuine commercial offer. Do not publish templated pages for UAE, Saudi Arabia, UK or other countries until the mill confirms its capability for that market.

## Ongoing buyer-guides — publish from evidence

Use Search Console queries and repeated sales questions to choose articles. Good candidates are comparisons and procurement questions, such as Steam versus Golden Sella, information required for a rice quotation, and how merchant exporters should prepare a mill brief.

Publish one useful guide at a time, link it to the relevant product and commercial page, then measure impressions, clicks and qualified WhatsApp leads for four weeks.
