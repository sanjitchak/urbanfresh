Requirement-led quality

Good rice starts with an agreed specification.

What counts as good rice changes by market and customer. We start by writing down the product and packing expectations that matter to your order.

What a useful rice specification covers

Variety and processing style are only the first layer. A serious buying conversation can also consider grain presentation, cooking expectations, broken tolerance, moisture expectations, polish, packaging, crop preference and destination-specific needs.

UrbanFresh does not display generic certificates, laboratory figures or fixed specifications without order context. Relevant documents and agreed parameters should be confirmed during the commercial process.

A practical quality flow

1. Capture the intended market

Retail shelves, food-service kitchens, wholesale trade and private labels can prioritise different outcomes. The intended use helps frame the product conversation.

2. Align the requested parameters

The buyer and supplier should confirm the rice variety, processing style, packaging format and other important specifications before commercial closure.

3. Confirm against the order

Availability, final specification, documentation, packing and dispatch details should be tied to the accepted order, not assumed from a website description.

4. Keep the dispatch brief consistent

The agreed commercial requirement should remain the reference point when packaging and fulfilment are coordinated.
